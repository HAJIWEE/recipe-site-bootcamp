Beef and Seafood Hor Fun

Serves 3-4

Ingredients

 Preperation Time: 20 mins

Cooking Time: 15 mins

Beef Marinade

  1. 1 tbsp light soy sauce
  2. 1 tbsp oyster sauce
  3. ½ tbsp sesame oil
  4. Pinch 5 spice
  5. Large pinch of sugar
  6. ½ tbsp corn flour

Other Ingredients

  1. Fresh / Dried Ho Fun noodles
  2. 1 onion
  3. 1 green pepper
  4. 200g rump steak
  5. 1 handful beansprouts
  6. 1 spring onion
  7. 1 tbsp black pepper sauce
  8. 1 tbsp oyster sauce
  9. 1 tbsp dark soy sauce
  10. Dash Sesame Oil
  11. The Beef Marinade

Instructions

Preparation Method

  1. Soak the dry noodles for 8-10 minutes (or until fully lost packet shape and each noodle strand is separate and softened) and then cool under cold water
  2. Finely slice the onion and pepper and place on the same dish
  3. Finely slice the spring onions and place on the side of the same dish
  4. Finely slice the beef and place in a small prep bowl
  5. Add the Beef Marinade ingredients to the sliced beef and mix well with one hand

Cooking Method

  1. Place 1-2 tablespoons vegetable oil in the wok on a high heat (smoking hot wok)
  2. Then cook the hor fun for 1 minute and season with a little dark soy
  3. Turn to medium heat and add the onions
  4. Once the onions are slightly softened, push to one side of the wok and then add the peppers
  5. Add 1 tbsp oil to the wok and bring back to a high heat
  6. Once smoking hot, add the beef and stir fry together for 1 minute on high heat
  7. If need be, push aside and add 1 tbsp oil to the base of the wok.
  8. Turn the heat back up to high and add the bean sprouts. Stir Fry for 30 seconds
  9. Push all the ingredients aside and now add the noodles
  10. Cover the noodles with 1-2 tbsp dark soy sauce
  11. Stir the noodles with a spoon whilst shaking the wok back and forth, until all the noodles are a consistent dark brown colour
  12. Add the spring onions and a drop of sesame oil
  13. Serve on a large dish, chilli sauce is a nice accompaniment

NOTES

*Sauté pan: If you happen to own a nonstick or cast-iron pan, I would recommend it for this recipe. But that said, any pan that you have can work — you may just have to be a bit more vigilant with stirring so that the rice and eggs don’t stick.
