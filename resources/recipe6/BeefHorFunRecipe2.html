Beef Hor Fun

A crowd pleaser at your local tze char stall, satisfy your cravings with this easy rice noodle recipe!

Ingredients

 Preperation Time: 10 mins

Cooking Time: 10 mins
  1. 200 g beef fillet (sliced across the grain)
  2. 300 g kway teow
  3. 2 stalks spring onion (cut into 4 cm lengths)
  4. 100 g bean sprouts
  5. 3 cloves garlic (minced)
  6. ½ yellow onion (sliced)
  7. 1 tbsp ginger juice
  8. 6 tbsp light soy sauce
  9. 2 tbsp dark soy sauce
  10. 1 TBsp Oyster Sauce
  11. 1 tsp seasame oil
  12. 1 Tsp white pepper
  13. 1 TBsp cornstarch
  14. vegetable Oil

Instructions

Preparation Method

  1. To get ginger juice, blend 1 to 2 inch ginger and strain juice
  2. In a bowl, combine beef, 2 tbsp light soy sauce, 1 tbsp dark soy sauce, ginger juice, 1 tsp white pepper powder, 1 tbsp cornstarch and 1 tbsp vegetable oil. Set aside until needed.

Cooking Method

  1. Sear beef slices In a pan on high heat, heat 3 tbsp vegetable oil and sear beef slices for 2 minutes. Remove from pan and set aside.
  2. Stir-fry garlic and onions In the same pan on high heat, heat another 4 tbsp vegetable oil and stir-fry garlic and onions for 2 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Add noodles, 4 tbsp light soy sauce, 1 Tbsp dark soy sauce, 2 Tbsp oyster sauce and 1 tsp sesame oil to the pan, stir-frying for 2 minutes. | Be sure to constantly stir while frying the noodles or it will get burnt and lumpy.
  4. In the same pan, toss in beef, bean sprouts and spring onions. Continue stir-frying another 2 minutes, until spring onions softens. | Substitute beef slices for any seafood of your choice such as prawns and squid.
  5. Toss in the bean sprouts and spring onions until they start to wilt.
  6. Transfer to a dish and garnish with some cut chilli (optional).

NOTES

*Sauté pan: If you happen to own a nonstick or cast-iron pan, I would recommend it for this recipe. But that said, any pan that you have can work — you may just have to be a bit more vigilant with stirring so that the rice and eggs don’t stick.
